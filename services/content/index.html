A Considered Fit

The Long Game Suits Some Firms
More Than Others

Content marketing rewards firms with a longer horizon. If immediate volume is the priority, paid media is the more honest first step. The notes below are meant to help you decide which season your firm is in.

A Good Fit If
  • You’re willing to invest for 6–9 months before the SEO compounding becomes obvious in your inbox. (After that, it rarely stops.)
  • You believe a prospect who reads three of your articles before booking a consultation is worth more than one who clicked an ad — because they are.
  • You want a website that does the “why us” conversation for you, not one that hands the visitor back to a contact form and hopes.
  • You have a few clear practice areas you genuinely want to be known for — not a list of twelve you list to look comprehensive.
Perhaps Not Yet If
  • You’re hoping for top-of-Google rankings within 30 days. In a competitive legal market those don’t exist for any firm honestly, and we won’t pretend otherwise.
  • You’d like to delegate content entirely and step away. Our process is collaborative because firm-specific nuance is what makes the work convert — if a fully hands-off model is what you need, a different agency is a better match.
  • You’re hoping AI alone will produce your firm’s editorial voice. We use AI tools, but the strategy, perspective, and authority come from people who understand your practice area.
  • You’d prefer content to publish without your review. Every word represents your firm, so we keep you in the approval loop — even when it adds a day to the timeline.

Who You Become

The Firm Whose Website Closes
Before the Phone Even Rings

Content marketing isn’t producing articles. It’s building a digital asset that pre-sells your firm in your sleep.

  • You become the firm Google trusts to answer the question.

    When someone in your jurisdiction types in your practice area, your articles appear — not because you bought your way in, but because Google has decided you’re the most authoritative answer. That trust transfers directly to the reader.

  • Your firm becomes its own lead source.

    Within 12 months, your largest source of new business stops being referrals or ads — it becomes organic search. Prospects find you, read three articles, fill out the form, and arrive at the consultation already convinced.

  • You become the firm other firms cite.

    When a deep article ranks well, other lawyers, journalists, and bloggers link to it. Every link is a vote of authority — both for Google and for the next prospect who finds you through a second-degree mention.

  • Your firm becomes a magnet for the right cases.

    Content lets you choose your future clientele. Write about complex commercial disputes and you’ll get complex commercial disputes. Write about flat-fee divorces and you’ll get flat-fee divorces. The inbox becomes a filter you control.

Here’s Exactly How This Works

A Disciplined Editorial System
Built for the Long Run

Content marketing fails when it’s sporadic. Here’s the system we run for every firm we work with — month after month, the same way.

  1. 01

    Editorial Strategy & Keyword Map (Weeks 1–3)

    We map your practice areas against actual search behaviour in your jurisdiction. You receive a 12-month editorial calendar of pillar articles, supporting posts, and conversion pages — sequenced for maximum SEO compounding.

  2. 02

    Website Copy Rewrite (Weeks 3–6)

    Before we publish a single article, we make sure the destination converts. Practice-area pages, attorney bios, the home page, the contact page — rewritten to move qualified visitors toward consultation, not just inform them.

  3. 03

    Monthly Article Production (Recurring)

    Four to six pieces per month: long-form pillar articles, fast-publish opinion pieces, and conversion-focused service pages. Each one researched, drafted by a legal-content specialist, reviewed by you, and optimised for both Google and human readers.

  4. 04

    Email Sequences & Nurture (Ongoing)

    We turn every form-fill into a 6–12 email educational sequence that warms the prospect, demonstrates your firm’s thinking, and brings them back to schedule when they’re ready — not when you’d like them to be.

  5. 05

    Quarterly Performance & Strategy Review

    Every 90 days: which articles are ranking, which are converting, which need refreshing, what the next quarter’s calendar looks like, and what the data is telling us about your prospect’s real questions. Editorial stays alive — never autopilot.
